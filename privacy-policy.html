Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Stromatic Media (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use stromatic.tech and our related services (collectively, the “Services”).

1. Who we are

Stromatic Media operates from Toronto, Ontario, Canada. For privacy inquiries: stromaticmedia@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

2.1 You provide directly

When you submit a contact form, service brief, or email us, we may collect name, email address, company name, billing details processed by our payment processor (Stripe), and the content of your messages or briefs.

2.2 Automatically

We may collect technical data such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, and pages visited. We use Plausible Analytics (cookieless, privacy-focused) to understand aggregate traffic patterns.

3. How we use information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide, fulfill, and support the Services you purchase;
  • Communicate about your orders, scheduling, and support;
  • Process payments and prevent fraud (via Stripe);
  • Improve the website and measure aggregate usage;
  • Comply with law and enforce our terms.

4. Legal bases (where applicable)

If you are in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, we rely on performance of a contract, legitimate interests (such as analytics and security), and consent where required.

5. Sharing and processors

We share data with service providers who assist our operations, including:

  • Stripe for payments and receipts;
  • Supabase for application backend and checkout orchestration;
  • Web forms provider for contact form delivery;
  • Plausible for privacy-friendly analytics.

We do not sell your personal information.

6. International transfers

We work with clients globally. Data may be processed in Canada and in countries where our processors operate. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards.

7. Retention

We retain information as long as needed to fulfill orders, meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Briefs and deliverables may be retained according to operational backup practices.

8. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect personal information. No method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure.

9. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal information, and to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Contact us to exercise these rights.

For Canadian residents: we handle personal information in accordance with applicable privacy laws including PIPEDA, where it applies.

10. Children

The Services are not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect their personal information.

11. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date will change when we do.
